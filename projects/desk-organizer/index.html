Desk Organizer

Multi-process fabrication across manual machining, CNC, sheet metal, and SLS printing

CNC MachiningSheet MetalSLS PrintingManual Mill/LatheLaser CuttingWood Working

A collection of fabricated objects built across multiple manufacturing processes, each designed to challenge a different production method.

The hammer was built on a manual lathe and mill. The nameplate was machined on a 3-axis CNC with turning on a Tormach lathe. The desk organizer features wood top panels, a sheet metal drawer (laser cut, brake formed, and spot welded), and a wooden key holder. The clock enclosure was laser cut from acrylic. An additional part was produced via SLS nylon printing.

By combining subtractive machining (manual and CNC milling/turning), form-fitting sheet metal work, and additive manufacturing (SLS), the project minimizes raw material waste while maintaining strict part-to-part tolerances across multiple material interfaces.
