Lego Rovers

Two Lego rover systems built for simulated Martian cargo delivery and disaster relief

Raspberry PiAutonomous SystemsSensorsPython

Two separate autonomous rover projects — MACRO and GEARS — each designed around a different mission profile, sensor stack, and navigation strategy.

MACRO (Mars Cargo Rover) simulates autonomous cargo delivery across rugged Martian terrain. The platform uses a Raspberry Pi on a Lego-bracket chassis with a rear motorized ramp and front-mounted battery as a counterweight. Five sensors handle full perceptual awareness: a front ultrasonic triggers emergency stops within 15 cm; dual IR line finders correct lateral drift; a center RGB color sensor manages forward velocity; and a front IMU counts magnetic sub-beacons to identify target drop-off sites. A priority control loop runs hazard detection → beacon counting → line tracking. A flood-fill pathfinding algorithm replaced an earlier left-hand rule for significantly faster completion times. Validated: speed tracking within 1.3% error, payload capacity up to 1029g without tipping, slope traversal up to 24°.

GEARS (Grand Emergency Aid Resource System) navigates disaster environments to deliver humanitarian aid and map terrain for secondary relief teams. The final chassis uses high-traction treads for zero-radius turning, driven by a Raspberry Pi. Sensors include front and right-mounted ultrasonics for obstacle avoidance and wall-following, a front IR sensor for thermal hazard detection (120W at 30 cm), and an under-chassis IMU detecting magnetic field spikes (10× ambient) simulating infrastructure faults. The software advances in precise 10 cm increments, logging a coordinate grid at each step and rolling back on hazard detection. On completion, the path matrix exports as a .csv for secondary teams. Validated: 13-minute mission time (vs. 18-minute target), 4 cm payload placement accuracy, 10 cm hazard clearance, 100% hazard mapping accuracy.

MACRO — Raspberry Pi core with ultrasonic, IR, color, and IMU sensors
GEARS — tread chassis with four-sensor SLAM stack and swivel-claw actuator
