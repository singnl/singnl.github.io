Purdue FSAE Fuel System

E85-compatible fuel system designed for Purdue FSAE PF25

Siemens NXFuel Systems

Designed the fuel system for Purdue FSAE's PF25 race car to accommodate E85 fuel for endurance events, replacing the previous 93 octane setup.

Fuel capacity was calculated from MoTeC i2 telemetry data from PF24's Michigan endurance run, which consumed approximately 5L on 93 octane. Since ethanol has roughly 33% less energy content than gasoline (per the U.S. Energy Information Administration), the equivalent E85 volume was calculated at 5L × 1.33 = 6.65L. A 10% safety margin brought the final target to 7.315L. The manufactured tank came in at approximately 7.65L, with size and location optimized for regulation compliance on the PF25 chassis.

Modeled in Siemens NX. The fuel system integrates a Yamaha R6 fuel rail with Bosch injectors, a Daschewerk fuel pump, Holley HydraMat, EFI fuel pressure regulator, and a fuel cell with custom internal baffles and chassis mounts.
